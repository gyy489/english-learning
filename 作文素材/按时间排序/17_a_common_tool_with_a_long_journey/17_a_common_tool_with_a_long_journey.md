## 正文

1. This afternoon, I read about Maori fish hooks in a quiet room.

   > 今天下午，我在一个安静的房间里读了关于毛利鱼钩的文章。
   >
2. At first, a fish hook looked like an ordinary tool to me.

   > 一开始，鱼钩在我看来像一个普通的工具。
   >
3. However, the story behind this small tool was much deeper than I expected.

   > 然而，这个小工具背后的故事比我预想的深得多。
   >
4. Maori people used wood, bone, stone, shell, and plant materials to prepare their hooks.

   > 毛利人用木头、骨头、石头、贝壳和植物材料来准备他们的鱼钩。
   >
5. These materials were common in daily life, but they were not easy to shape.

   > 这些材料在日常生活中很常见，但它们并不容易塑形。
   >
6. A fisherman had to practise carefully before he could make a useful hook.

   > 一个渔民必须认真练习，才能做出有用的鱼钩。
   >
7. He might dry the wood, tie pieces together, and move his hands gently.

   > 他可能会晒干木头，把不同部分绑在一起，并轻轻地移动双手。
   >
8. If his hands began to shake, the hook could become weak or uneven.

   > 如果他的手开始摇晃，鱼钩可能会变得脆弱或不平整。
   >
9. This work was not like using a spoon to stir a cup of tea.

   > 这项工作不像用勺子搅拌一杯茶那样简单。
   >
10. It needed patience, skill, and a clear idea of the fish in the sea.

> 它需要耐心、技巧，以及对海中鱼类的清楚认识。

11. Some early Europeans argued that the Maori hooks looked strange.

> 一些早期欧洲人争辩说，毛利鱼钩看起来很奇怪。

12. They did not understand why a round hook could catch fish well.

> 他们不明白为什么圆形鱼钩能很好地捕鱼。

13. Later, a museum study showed that the design was clever and useful.

> 后来，一项博物馆研究表明，这种设计既聪明又实用。

14. The hook could turn and slide into the right place in the fish's mouth.

> 鱼钩可以转动，并滑到鱼嘴里合适的位置。

15. This result can remind us not to judge an old tool too quickly.

> 这个结果可以提醒我们，不要太快判断一种古老工具。

16. Something may look simple, but it may carry a long journey of thinking.

> 有些东西看起来简单，却可能承载着很长的思考过程。

17. The journey of the hook also reached a new stage when European trade grew.

> 当欧洲贸易发展时，鱼钩的历程也到达了一个新阶段。

18. Metal tools came across the sea and became easier to get.

> 金属工具跨海而来，并变得更容易获得。

19. Some old materials fell out of daily use because metal was stronger.

> 一些旧材料退出了日常使用，因为金属更坚固。

20. At the same time, people did not forget every traditional idea.

> 与此同时，人们并没有忘记每一个传统观念。

21. Some fish hooks became objects for art, memory, or even a small ceremony.

> 一些鱼钩变成了艺术、记忆，甚至小型仪式中的物品。

22. They were no longer only useful tools for food.

> 它们不再只是获取食物的实用工具。

23. They also showed family memory, local skill, and respect for the sea.

> 它们也展示了家族记忆、地方技艺和对海洋的尊重。

24. In some places, a fish hook could have a religious meaning as well.

> 在一些地方，鱼钩也可能具有宗教意义。

25. This made the debate about old and new tools more interesting.

> 这使关于新旧工具的讨论更加有趣。

26. If a new tool is useful, people may welcome it warmly.

> 如果一种新工具有用，人们可能会热情地欢迎它。

27. But if an old tool holds culture, people may still protect it.

> 但是，如果一种旧工具承载着文化，人们可能仍然会保护它。

28. I thought of leaves in a garden after reading this story.

> 读完这个故事后，我想到了花园里的叶子。

29. Some leaves look the same from far away, but each one has its own shape.

> 有些叶子从远处看很相似，但每一片都有自己的形状。

30. In the same way, a common tool can have its own history.

> 同样，一个常见的工具也可以有自己的历史。

31. It is harmful to think that modern things are always better than old things.

> 认为现代事物总是比旧事物好是有害的。

32. This article kept me awake because it showed how culture can live inside a small object.

> 这篇文章让我保持清醒，因为它展示了文化如何存在于一个小物件之中。
