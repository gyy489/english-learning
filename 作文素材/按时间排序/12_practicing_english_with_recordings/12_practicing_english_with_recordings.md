## 正文

1. This week, I tried a new way to practice my English speaking.

   > 这周，我尝试了一种练习英语口语的新方法。
   >
2. Instead of only reading silently, I decided to record my voice every night.

   > 我不再只是默读，而是决定每天晚上录下（record）自己的声音。
   >
3. I gave myself one simple restriction: I could only read the same short paragraph three times.

   > 我给自己设定了一个简单的限制（restriction）：同一篇短文只能读三遍。
   >
4. This small rule helped me focus more carefully on each sentence.

   > 这个小规则帮助我更认真地专注于每一个句子。
   >
5. At first, the practice felt quite uncomfortable.

   > 一开始，这个练习让我感到相当（quite）不舒服。
   >
6. When I looked at the blank recording screen, I often had moments of doubt.

   > 当我看着空白的（blank）录音界面时，我经常会有怀疑（doubt）的时刻。
   >
7. I worried that my pronunciation was unclear and that I could not communicate my ideas naturally.

   > 我担心自己的发音不清楚，也担心自己不能自然地表达（communicate）想法。
   >
8. Sometimes the process felt frustrating because mistakes appeared again and again.

   > 有时候这个过程让人沮丧（frustrating），因为错误一次又一次出现（appeared）。
   >
9. However, listening to my own recordings brought an unexpected result.

   > 然而，听自己的录音带来了一个意想不到的（unexpected）结果。
   >
10. I noticed a clear contrast between what I thought I had said and what I actually said.

    > 我注意到自己以为说出来的内容和实际说出来的内容之间有明显的反差（contrast）。
    >
11. Some words sounded too weak, while other words were spoken too quickly.

    > 有些单词听起来太弱，而另一些单词又说得太快。
    >
12. These problems had appeared unpredictably before, but the recordings made them easier to find.

    > 这些问题以前总是不可预测地（unpredictably）出现，但录音让它们更容易被发现。
    >
13. Gradually, I learned not to force myself to sound perfect.

    > 渐渐地（gradually），我学会了不强迫（force）自己听起来完美。
    >
14. I also stopped feeling disappointed every time I made a small mistake.

    > 我也不再每次犯小错误时都感到失望（disappointed）。
    >
15. Instead, I described the problem in my notebook and wrote short reminders for the next practice.

    > 相反，我会在笔记本里描述（described）问题，并为下一次练习写下简短的提醒（reminders）。
    >
16. For example, I wrote, “Slow down,” “Open your mouth more,” and “Do not rush the ending sounds.”

    > 例如，我写下：“慢一点”，“嘴巴张开一点”，以及“不要急着读完结尾音”。
    >
17. The practice was ordinary, but it changed how I listened to myself.

    > 这个练习很普通（ordinary），但它改变了我聆听自己的方式。
    >
18. I became more aware of my surroundings, especially quiet sounds and background noise during recording.

    > 我更加注意周围环境（surroundings），尤其是录音时的细小声音和背景噪音。
    >
19. I also realized that improvement does not happen through endless practice without thinking.

    > 我也意识到，进步不是通过无休止的（endless）练习却不思考来实现的。
    >
20. It grows when I listen carefully, accept uncertainty, and make small changes every day.

    > 它来自认真倾听、接受不确定性（uncertainty），以及每天做出小小的改变。
    >
