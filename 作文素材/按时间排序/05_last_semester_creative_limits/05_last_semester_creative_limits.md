# Creative Work With Limits

- 时间线索：Last semester
- 来源：主题2 / 第 4 篇

1. Last semester, I tried an unusual experiment during one of my personal art projects.  
    > 上个学期，我在自己的一个个人艺术项目（art project）中尝试了一项不同寻常的实验（experiment）。  
1. Instead of using many digital tools, colors, and effects, I limited myself to only a black pen and a small sketchbook.  
    > 我没有使用大量数字工具（digital tools）、颜色（colors）和特效（effects），而是把自己限制（limit）在只使用一支黑色钢笔和一本小速写本（sketchbook）。  
1. At first, this restriction felt frustrating because I was used to having many options while working.  
    > 一开始，这种限制（restriction）让我感到沮丧（frustrating），因为我已经习惯了在创作时拥有很多选择（options）。  
1. Whenever I made a mistake, I wanted to change or correct everything immediately.  
    > 每当我犯错（make a mistake）时，我都会想立刻修改（correct）所有东西。  

⸻

1. However, after several days, I noticed that the limitation was affecting my work in an unexpected way.  
    > 然而，几天之后，我注意到这种限制（limitation）正在以一种意想不到的（unexpected）方式影响（affect）我的创作。  
1. Since I could not rely on complex tools or endless editing, I started paying more attention to simple shapes, composition, and contrast.  
    > 由于我不能依赖（rely on）复杂工具或无止境的修改（editing），我开始更加关注简单的形状（shapes）、构图（composition）和对比（contrast）。  
1. I also spent more time thinking carefully before drawing each line.  
    > 而且，我在画每一条线之前，都会花更多时间认真思考。  
1. As a result, the process became slower, but my attention became much deeper.  
    > 结果（as a result）是，整个过程变慢了，但我的专注（attention）却变得更加深入（deeper）。  

⸻

1. One surprising change was emotional rather than technical.  
    > 一个令人惊讶（surprising）的变化是情感上的（emotional），而不是技术上的（technical）。  
1. Normally, I often judged my work too quickly and became disappointed when the result was imperfect.  
    > 通常，我经常过快地评价（judge）自己的作品，并且在结果不完美（imperfect）时感到失望（disappointed）。  
1. During this experiment, though, I gradually became more comfortable with uncertainty and mistakes.  
    > 不过，在这次实验过程中，我逐渐（gradually）开始更能接受（be comfortable with）不确定性（uncertainty）和错误。  
1. I realized that creative work does not always need complete control.  
    > 我意识到，创造性工作（creative work）并不总是需要完全的控制（control）。  
1. Sometimes limitations can force a person to discover new methods and ideas.  
    > 有时候，限制（limitations）反而会迫使（force）一个人发现新的方法（methods）和想法（ideas）。  

⸻

1. Now, I think that having too many choices can occasionally weaken creativity instead of improving it.  
    > 现在，我认为太多选择（choices）有时（occasionally）反而会削弱（weaken）创造力，而不是提升它。  
1. Restrictions may seem negative at first, but they can sometimes help people focus more clearly on what truly matters.  
    > 限制（restrictions）一开始可能看起来是负面的（negative），但它们有时反而能帮助人们更加清晰地专注于（focus on）真正重要（truly matters）的东西。
