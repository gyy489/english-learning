## 正文

1. In 2010, a small portrait stirred great excitement in the art world.

   > 2010年，一幅小型肖像画在艺术界引起了巨大轰动。
   >

2. It had been bought at an auction in Switzerland for a modest sum.

   > 它是在瑞士的一场拍卖会上以不高的价格购得的。
   >

3. The young woman in the picture was believed to be Bianca Sforza.

   > 人们认为画中的年轻女子是比安卡·斯福尔扎。
   >

4. She was the daughter of Ludovico Sforza, the Duke of Milan.

   > 她是米兰公爵卢多维科·斯福尔扎的女儿。
   >

5. Some experts argue that Leonardo da Vinci created the work.

   > 一些专家主张这幅作品出自列奥纳多·达·芬奇之手。
   >

6. If the portrait is authentic, it could change our understanding of Leonardo’s art.

   > 如果这幅肖像画是真迹，它可能会改变我们对达·芬奇艺术的理解。
   >

7. A Canadian art collector named Peter Silverman brought the picture to public attention.

   > 一位名叫彼得·西尔弗曼的加拿大艺术收藏家使这幅画受到公众关注。
   >

8. At first, it seemed to be a normal portrait from the Renaissance.

   > 起初，它看起来只是一幅普通的文艺复兴时期肖像画。
   >

9. However, Silverman’s sharp eyes noticed the soft shadows and carefully drawn hair.

   > 然而，西尔弗曼敏锐的眼睛注意到了柔和的阴影和精心绘制的头发。
   >

10. These details appeared to tie the picture to Leonardo’s style.

   > 这些细节似乎将这幅画与达·芬奇的风格联系起来。
   >

11. Silverman therefore asked art expert Martin Kemp to study it.

   > 因此，西尔弗曼请艺术专家马丁·肯普研究这幅画。
   >

12. Kemp found that the woman’s clothes and hairstyle matched Milanese fashion in the 1490s.

   > 肯普发现，这名女子的服装和发型符合15世纪90年代米兰的时尚。
   >

13. The artist had also blended light and dark areas without clear, hard edges.

   > 画家还将明暗区域融合在一起，没有明显而生硬的边缘。
   >

14. This gentle technique was often used by Leonardo.

   > 达·芬奇经常使用这种柔和的技法。
   >

15. The picture was drawn on vellum, a special material made from calfskin.

   > 这幅画绘制在羊皮纸上，这是一种由小牛皮制成的特殊材料。
   >

16. Workers made vellum by stretching and drying the skin until it became smooth.

   > 工匠们通过拉伸并晒干兽皮，使其变得光滑，从而制成羊皮纸。
   >

17. Artists of that period could make black material from a burned bone or a dry branch.

   > 那个时期的画家可以用烧过的骨头或干树枝制作黑色绘画材料。
   >

18. During a scientific test, a worker may stir a tiny colour sample with a small spoon.

   > 在科学检测中，工作人员可能会用小勺搅拌微量的颜色样本。
   >

19. Special cameras revealed several weak fingerprint marks on the picture.

   > 特殊相机显示出画面上有几处模糊的指纹痕迹。
   >

20. The marks had been left before the layer of paint became dry.

   > 这些痕迹是在颜料层变干之前留下的。
   >

21. One print partly matched a fingerprint found in Leonardo’s notebooks.

   > 其中一枚指纹与达·芬奇笔记本中的一枚指纹部分吻合。
   >

22. This discovery provided interesting evidence, although it did not prove authorship.

   > 这一发现提供了有趣的证据，但并未证明作者身份。
   >

23. An analysis of the colours showed that they were available in the late fifteenth century.

   > 对颜色的分析表明，它们在15世纪晚期已经可以获得。
   >

24. Nevertheless, the claim that Leonardo painted the portrait remains controversial.

   > 尽管如此，关于这幅肖像画由达·芬奇创作的说法仍存在争议。
   >

25. Critics say that the work does not show enough movement or fresh ideas.

   > 批评者认为，这幅作品没有表现出足够的动感或新颖思想。
   >

26. They believe it may have been produced by one of Leonardo’s pupils.

   > 他们认为它可能是达·芬奇的一名学生创作的。
   >

27. As a result, many experts are still on the fence.

   > 因此，许多专家仍然持观望态度。
   >

28. Historical records show that Bianca married a Milanese courtier at a young age.

   > 历史记录显示，比安卡年纪很小时便嫁给了一位米兰宫廷官员。
   >

29. Sadly, she died at the age of fourteen, possibly because of problems during childbirth.

   > 令人难过的是，她在十四岁时去世，原因可能是分娩时出现的问题。
   >

30. The quiet sadness in her face suggests that the picture may commemorate her short life.

   > 她脸上平静的悲伤表明，这幅画可能是为了纪念她短暂的一生。
   >

31. Modern tools can study a painting, but they cannot always reach a final answer.

   > 现代工具可以研究一幅画，却不一定能得出最终答案。
   >

32. Scholars must chew over scientific results, historical facts, and artistic style together.

   > 学者们必须综合仔细考虑科学结果、历史事实和艺术风格。
   >

33. Careful checks are important because valuable works often enter the international art trade.

   > 细致的检查非常重要，因为珍贵作品经常进入国际艺术品交易市场。
   >

34. Museums must also protect old pictures from soil, dust, and harmful chemicals.

   > 博物馆还必须保护古老画作免受泥土、灰尘和有害化学物质的影响。
   >

35. Under a round magnifying lens, even the smallest mark can become an important clue.

   > 在圆形放大镜下，即使最细小的痕迹也可能成为重要线索。
   >

36. Whether or not Leonardo painted it, the portrait continues to attract people around the world.

   > 无论它是否出自达·芬奇之手，这幅肖像画仍在吸引世界各地的人们。
   >
