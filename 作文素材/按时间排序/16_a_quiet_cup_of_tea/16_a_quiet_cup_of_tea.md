# A Quiet Cup of Tea

- 天数：第 16 天
- 来源真题：A Brief History of Tea 茶叶简史
- 来源文件：雅思真题/Markdown资料/7月阅读/ReadingPractice/PDF/1. P1 - A Brief History of Tea 茶叶简史.pdf.md
- 复习内容：D15 生词复用 + IELTS 阅读主题改写

## 复习生词

- slide - 滑动；滑倒
- glad - 高兴的；欣慰的
- hold - 拿着；握住；容纳
- through - 穿过；通过

## 正文

1. This morning, I made a cup of tea before I started studying.

   > 今天早上，我在开始学习之前泡了一杯茶。
   >
2. I held the warm cup in my hands and felt more awake.

   > 我双手捧着（held）温热的杯子，感觉更清醒了。
   >
3. Tea may look simple, but it has a long journey behind it.

   > 茶看起来可能很简单，但它背后有一段很长的旅程。
   >
4. In old stories, tea began when leaves fell into hot water by chance.

   > 在古老的故事里，茶是因为树叶偶然掉进热水中而开始的。
   >
5. The smell went through the water slowly and changed its colour.

   > 香味慢慢进入（through）水中，并改变了水的颜色。
   >
6. People liked the fresh taste, so the drink became more popular.

   > 人们喜欢这种清新的味道，所以这种饮品变得更受欢迎。
   >
7. Later, tea became connected with quiet thinking and religious life.

   > 后来，茶和安静思考以及宗教生活联系在一起。
   >
8. Some people drank it before meditation because it helped them stay calm.

   > 有些人在冥想前喝茶，因为它帮助他们保持平静。
   >
9. In Japan, making tea slowly became a careful ceremony.

   > 在日本，泡茶慢慢变成了一种细致的仪式。
   >
10. The host had to move gently, speak politely, and prepare everything with care.

    > 主人需要动作轻柔，说话礼貌，并认真准备每一个细节。
    >
11. A small mistake could make the spoon slide or the cup shake.

    > 一个小错误就可能让勺子滑动（slide）或让杯子晃动。
    >
12. This shows that tea was not only a drink, but also a way to practise patience.

    > 这说明茶不只是一种饮品，也是一种练习耐心的方式。
    >
13. When tea reached Europe, many rich people saw it as something special.

    > 当茶传到欧洲时，许多富人把它看作一种特别的东西。
    >
14. At first, it was expensive, so ordinary families could not buy it easily.

    > 起初，茶很贵，所以普通家庭不容易买到它。
    >
15. As more tea was brought by trade, the price became lower.

    > 随着更多茶叶通过贸易被带来，价格变低了。
    >
16. People began to drink it at home, in shops, and even in gardens.

    > 人们开始在家里、商店里，甚至花园里喝茶。
    >
17. Some doctors argued about whether tea was healthy or harmful.

    > 一些医生争论茶到底是健康的还是有害的。
    >
18. However, many people ignored the debate and continued to enjoy their cups.

    > 然而，很多人没有理会这场争论，继续享受自己的茶。
    >
19. I am glad that a small drink can carry so much history.

    > 我很高兴（glad）一种小小的饮品可以承载这么多历史。
    >
20. When I drink tea now, I do not only taste it; I also think about the people who carried it across countries and cultures.

    > 现在我喝茶时，我不只是在品尝它；我也会想到那些把茶带过不同国家和文化的人。
    >
21. A drink can travel like a story, because every place adds a new meaning to it.

    > 一种饮品可以像故事一样传播，因为每个地方都会给它增加新的意义。
    >
22. In China, tea was first linked with daily health and careful living.

    > 在中国，茶最早和日常健康以及谨慎生活联系在一起。
    >
23. In Japan, it became part of art, manners, and peaceful rooms.

    > 在日本，茶变成了艺术、礼仪和安静空间的一部分。
    >
24. In Europe, it first showed wealth, but later it became a common habit.

    > 在欧洲，茶一开始显示财富，但后来变成了一种普通习惯。
    >
25. This change tells us that culture does not stay in one fixed place.

    > 这种变化告诉我们，文化不会停留在一个固定的地方。
    >
26. It moves through trade, travel, conversation, and ordinary family life.

    > 它会通过（through）贸易、旅行、交谈和普通家庭生活移动。
    >
27. Sometimes a simple object can hold the memory of many countries.

    > 有时，一个简单的物品可以承载（hold）许多国家的记忆。
    >
28. A cup of tea can hold a legend, a ceremony, a debate, and a friendship.

    > 一杯茶可以承载一个传说、一场仪式、一次争论和一段友谊。
    >
29. I used to think history only lived in books and museums.

    > 我过去以为历史只存在于书本和博物馆里。
    >
30. Now I understand that history can also sit quietly on my desk.

    > 现在我明白，历史也可以安静地放在我的书桌上。
    >
31. When I feel tired, I am glad to pause and notice these small things.

    > 当我感到疲惫时，我很高兴（glad）能停下来注意这些小事。
    >
32. A quiet cup of tea can remind me that learning also grows slowly, one day at a time.

    > 一杯安静的茶可以提醒我，学习也是慢慢成长的，一天一点。
    >
